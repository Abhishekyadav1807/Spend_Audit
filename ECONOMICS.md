# Unit Economics

The economic model for AISpendAudit depends on whether it creates qualified conversations, not raw traffic. I am assuming Credex earns margin by selling discounted AI infrastructure credits sourced below retail. If a startup buys $30,000/year of credits through Credex and Credex keeps a 12-18% gross margin, one converted customer is worth roughly $3,600-$5,400 gross profit before support. I will use $4,500 as a working average gross profit per converted customer.

For zero-budget GTM, cash CAC is close to $0, but labor CAC matters. If one founder/operator spends 15 hours in a week and values time at $50/hour, the week costs $750. A realistic week-one funnel from the GTM plan is 250 visitors, 45 completed audits, 18 leads, 5 consultations, and 1 purchase. That makes CAC about $750 for one customer, leaving roughly $3,750 gross profit on the first converted account. If the same content and case studies keep producing two customers per month with 20 hours of monthly maintenance, CAC trends closer to $500 per customer.

Channel assumptions:

- Direct founder/CTO outreach: 40 messages -> 8 audits -> 3 leads -> 1 call. Labor CAC around $250 per booked call.
- Show HN / Indie Hackers launch: 150 visitors -> 30 audits -> 10 leads -> 2 calls. Labor CAC around $150 per booked call if the post takes 3 hours.
- Anonymized teardown posts: 60 visitors/post -> 12 audits -> 4 leads -> 1 call. Labor CAC around $100 per booked call once the format is repeatable.

The funnel becomes profitable if 35% of completed audits become leads, 25% of leads book a consultation, and 20% of consultations convert to a credit purchase. With 1,000 completed audits, that produces 350 leads, 88 calls, and 18 customers. At $4,500 gross profit per customer, that is $81,000 gross profit before tool and labor costs.

For this tool to drive $1M ARR in 18 months, several things must be true. Credex would need either 222 customers at $4,500/year gross profit equivalent or fewer larger customers with higher credit volume. A plausible path is 12,000 completed audits over 18 months, 30% lead capture (3,600 leads), 20% consultation booking (720 calls), and 12% call-to-purchase conversion (86 customers). To reach $1M ARR from only 86 customers, average annual credit volume must be much higher: about $11,600 gross profit per customer. That likely means the tool must attract later seed/Series A teams spending $8k-$50k/month on AI infrastructure, not only tiny subscription users.

The biggest sensitivity is qualified spend. A 2-person startup saving $40/month is a useful user but not a Credex customer. The product should stay honest for low-savings users while making the >$500/month savings path very easy to book.
